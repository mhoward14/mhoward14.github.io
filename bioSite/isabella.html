ISABELLA'S STORY

A Journey from Germany to America

Her Journey

Introduction

Isabella was born in May 2012 in Fort Walton Beach, FL. Six months later, her family received orders to Geilenkirchen, Germany, where they lived on the economy with no U.S. base support. Most of her early life was spent in Germany, where she attended both German and international schools with children from 17 different nations assigned to NATO Air Base Geilenkirchen.

What She Loved About Germany

Isabella has wonderful memories of Germany. She loved visiting Lego Land and Dardledome indoor playground. Her favorite experiences included attending festivals like the Lantern Festival, Fasching, Karneval (Mardi Gras), and the Christmas Markets. She also cherished visits to the Tier Park—a wild, natural zoo in the woods—and playing in the many parks throughout the region.

Germany vs. America

Moving to America brought significant changes for Isabella. She noticed Germany was always rainy while Colorado is sunny and dry. The houses are different—their old German home had many bricks and a small kitchen, plus a sheep pasture outside. Now she enjoys reconnecting with extended family, exploring new parks, and adapting to her new school in America.

A Typical Five Year Old

Isabella is a bright and joyful five-year-old girl. She loves playing with dolls and stuffed animals, reading stories, and learning new things every day. She's growing more independent and making new friends easily. Her school is wonderful, and she's becoming fluent in English while maintaining her German heritage. She's a joy to be around and loved by everyone who knows her.

World Traveler

Growing Up Between Worlds

Isabella's childhood is unique—she's experienced the rich cultures of both Germany and America. This exposure has given her a global perspective at a young age. She speaks both German and English, understands different customs and traditions, and has friends from around the world. These experiences shape her into a well-rounded, culturally aware child.

Family Love

Family & Future

Isabella's greatest joy is being with her family. Whether in Germany or America, family time is what matters most. She's learning to be adaptable, resilient, and open-minded. As she grows, these qualities will serve her well. Her unique background and experiences will help her become a compassionate, globally-minded individual ready to explore the world.
